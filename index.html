One of My Passions: Swimming

Since early childhood, swimming has been a significant part of my life. It has not only provided me with a way to stay fit and healthy but also taught me the value of discipline, perseverance, and teamwork. Whether I was swimming competivtively or just for fun, it has been something that brings calmness and joy into my life and is something that I hope to continue doing for the rest of my life.

My swim workouts typically follow steps like these:

  1. Warm up with easy swimming and drills.
    • 100 Swim
    • 100 Kick
    • 100 Individual Medley
    • 100 Pull
  2. Complete technique-focused sets for each stroke.
    • 2 x 50 Fingertip glides
    • 2 x 50 Catch-up
    • 2 x 50 Underwater Pullouts
  3. Perform endurance or aerobic training sets.
    • 4 x 200 Freestyle
    • 4 x 200 Stroke of Choice
  4. Swim sprint intervals to build speed and power.
    • 8 X 25 Free every 30 seconds
    • 4 x 50 Free every 50-60 seconds
  5. Finish with a cooldown to promote recovery.
    • 100 Swim
    • 100 Choice or Kick set

Benefits of Training All Four Strokes

Having swam my entire life, I have come to value the necessity to vary my training routine to include all four strokes. This approach ensures that I develop a well-rounded skill set that allows me to balance out my training and muscle usage and prevents overuse injuries. While I have favorite strokes, being able to train all strokes has helped me become a more versatile and resilient swimmer and helped me grow in my swimming abilities.
